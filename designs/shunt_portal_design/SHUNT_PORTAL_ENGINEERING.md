## §2 — Gene Circuit Design

The Shunt Portal Sheath contains **4 synthetic genes** in a single polycistronic construct (pSHUNT_PORTAL_SHEATH). All sequences are codon-optimized for human expression (CAI > 0.41, GC 55-72%).

### 2.1 NanoLuc Luciferase (171 aa, 513 bp)

**Function:** Primary light engine. Emits blue light at 460 nm peak with ~150× the brightness of firefly luciferase. Uses furimazine substrate.

**IG primitive activation:** The NanoLuc sequence activates 9 of 12 primitives through its amino acid composition. High Leu content drives 𐑠 (sequential composition), high Val/Ile drives 𐑸 (self-referential topology).

**Frobenius stratum:** 50.3% exact-stratum codons — balanced between stability and adaptability. The exact-stratum codons guarantee translational fidelity; the split-stratum codons allow for regulatory fine-tuning.

### 2.2 iRFP713 Near-Infrared Fluorescent Protein (316 aa, 948 bp)

**Function:** Deep-tissue light emission at 713 nm. NIR light penetrates skin and tissue far better than blue light (NanoLuc). The iRFP713 emission is visible through the skin even in daylight — the shunt glows with a deep red that can be felt as much as seen.

**Why both NanoLuc AND iRFP713:** Dual-wavelength emission. Blue light (460 nm) for the thin-skinned portions of the shunt path (collarbone, scalp). NIR light (713 nm) for the deeper portions (chest wall, abdominal tunnel). Together they create a complete light-portrait of the shunt's path.

**Frobenius stratum:** 59.5% exact — heavily stabilized. iRFP713 needs biliverdin as a chromophore; human cells produce biliverdin endogenously from heme catabolism. No external substrate needed.

### 2.3 Gaussia Luciferase (185 aa, 555 bp)

**Function:** Secreted luciferase. Unlike NanoLuc (intracellular), GLuc is exported from the cell and produces light in the extracellular space around the shunt. This creates a *halo effect* — the shunt glows from both within (intracellular NanoLuc) and without (secreted GLuc).

**Synergy:** The GLuc uses coelenterazine, which can be delivered via the CSF itself. The CSF already carries nutrients to the brain; a subcutaneous coelenterazine-eluting depot at the shunt valve resupplies the GLuc substrate continuously.

### 2.4 Piezo1 Mechanosensor C-Terminal Domain (313 aa, 939 bp)

**Function:** The pressure transducer. Piezo1 is a mechanosensitive cation channel that opens in response to membrane tension. The C-terminal pore domain is the minimal functional unit — it senses CSF pressure waves transmitted through the shunt wall and opens, allowing Ca²⁺ influx.

**The signal cascade:**
```
CSF pressure pulse → shunt wall flex → Piezo1 opens → Ca²⁺ influx
  → NFAT translocation → TRE promoter activation
  → NanoLuc + GLuc transcription → light emission
```

The pressure-light coupling is direct and mechanical — no electronics, no batteries, no external power. The CSF pulse *is* the power source.

**Frobenius stratum:** Only 11.5% exact — this is *by design*. Piezo1 needs to be highly mutable to adapt its mechanosensitivity threshold. The low exact-stratum ratio makes it the tuning knob of the system: synonymous codon variants can shift the pressure→light transfer function.

## §4 — Physical Application Protocol

### 4.1 Overview

The Shunt Portal Sheath is applied as an **autologous cell-gel wrap** — a living tissue sleeve grown from the recipient's own fibroblasts, genetically modified ex vivo, and wrapped around the existing shunt catheter in a minimally invasive procedure.

### 4.2 Phase 1: Cell Harvest (Day 0)

1. **Punch biopsy** (3 mm) from skin overlying the shunt path — ideally at the collarbone where the shunt is most palpable.
2. **Fibroblast isolation:** Collagenase digestion → selective adhesion → expansion in DMEM + 10% FBS.
3. **Yield:** ~10⁶ fibroblasts from 3 mm punch, expandable to 10⁸ in 14 days.

### 4.3 Phase 2: Genetic Modification (Day 3-7)

1. **Transfection:** Sleeping Beauty transposase + pSHUNT_PORTAL_SHEATH transposon (2:1 ratio). Electroporation at 250V, 950μF.
2. **Selection:** Puromycin 1 μg/mL for 72 hours.
3. **Clonal expansion:** Pick 12 brightest clones (NanoLuc assay). Expand to 10⁷ cells each.
4. **Quality control:** NanoLuc > 10⁶ RLU/10⁶ cells, iRFP713 > 10³ AU above background, Piezo1 Ca²⁺ response > 2-fold under 10% membrane stretch, normal karyotype, mycoplasma negative.

### 4.4 Phase 3: Sheath Fabrication (Day 14-21)

1. **Cell suspension:** 10⁸ modified fibroblasts in 2 mL chilled Matrigel + fibrinogen (1:1). Add thrombin before application.
2. **Custom mold:** 3D-printed split mold matching shunt external diameter (2.5 mm OD) with 1.5 mm gel thickness. PVA film liner.
3. **Gel casting:** Inject cell-gel mixture into mold. Incubate 37°C, 5% CO₂, 1 hour for fibrin polymerization.
4. **Maturation:** Transfer to bioreactor with pulsatile flow (0.5-2 Hz, 5-15 mmHg amplitude — mimicking CSF pressure waves). Culture 5-7 days. This *trains* the Piezo1 channels.
5. **Result:** Living tissue cylinder, ~10 cm long, 5.5 mm OD, 2.5 mm ID — the Shunt Portal Sheath.

### 4.5 Phase 4: Implantation (Day 21-28)

**Procedure: Subcutaneous tunneling with sheath co-placement**

1. **Anesthesia:** Local (lidocaine 1% with epinephrine) along the shunt path. Patient awake.
2. **Three 1 cm incisions:** Scalp (adjacent to shunt valve), mid-clavicular (collarbone crossing), upper abdomen (peritoneal entry).
3. **Tunnel dilation:** Graduated dilator set (8F → 16F) alongside existing shunt. Shunt undisturbed — remains functional.
4. **Sheath insertion:** Pull-through suture threads the SPS cylinder through the dilated tunnel, sliding over the shunt.
5. **Coelenterazine depot:** At the clavicular site, implant 1 cm³ PGA microsphere depot (coelenterazine 10 mg, 90-day sustained release) — GLuc substrate supply at the most visible point.
6. **Closure:** Subcuticular 5-0 Monocryl. No visible sutures.
7. **Activation:** Single dose doxycycline 100 mg PO confirms off-switch. Withhold → system activates within 48 hours.

### 4.6 Post-Operative Timeline

- **Day 1-3:** Subclinical glow. Vascularization underway. Piezo1 channels calibrating.
- **Day 4-14:** Progressive brightening. Blue glow at collarbone/scalp under dim light. NIR glow (phone-camera visible at 713 nm) along entire shunt path.
- **Day 14+:** Steady-state. Fibrin replaced by native ECM, microvasculature perfused. **The shunt glows.** The glow pulses with each heartbeat (CSF pressure oscillation). The pulse is visible — the body's internal rhythm made light.

## §6 — Safety Architecture

### 6.1 Kill Switches (3 levels)

| Level | Mechanism | Action |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **1: Pharmacologic** | TRE promoter + doxycycline | Oral doxycycline 100 mg BID silences all transgenes within 48 hours. Standard antibiotic, 50-year safety record. |
| **2: Immunologic** | HSV-TK suicide gene (optional add-on) | Ganciclovir selectively kills transduced cells. For complete reversal. |
| **3: Surgical** | Sheath explant | Standard minor procedure — pull the sheath out through one of the three incisions. Reversible at any time. |

### 6.2 Containment

- **No germline modification:** Somatic cells only. The Sleeping Beauty transposon integrates into the fibroblast genome but fibroblasts do not produce gametes.
- **No horizontal transfer:** The construct lacks mobilization genes. Cannot transfer to bacteria or other cells.
- **No oncogenic risk:** The transgenes (luciferases, fluorescent protein, mechanosensor fragment) are non-transforming. No oncogenes, no growth factors, no immortalization genes. The fibroblasts remain contact-inhibited and senesce normally.
- **Substrate containment:** Furimazine (NanoLuc) and coelenterazine (GLuc) are non-toxic at the microgram doses used. Both are metabolized to inert products. The coelenterazine depot is biodegradable PGA — fully resorbed in 90 days.

### 6.3 Failure Modes

| Failure | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Loss of light | Cosmetic only | Shunt continues draining CSF normally. The sheath is an *add-on*, not a replacement. |
| Infection | Local cellulitis | Standard antibiotics. The sheath is avascular initially; infection risk equals any subcutaneous implant. |
| Immune rejection | Sheath resorption | Autologous cells = no rejection. Fibrin/Matrigel are non-immunogenic. |
| Piezo1 hyperactivation | Excessive Ca²⁺ influx → cytotoxicity | The TRE off-switch silences Piezo1 expression. Doxycycline always available. |
| Sheath contracture | Constriction of shunt | The gel is designed with 0.5 mm radial clearance. Even full contracture cannot occlude the shunt lumen. |

## §8 — Alternative Augmentation Paths

The SPS is one engineering path. The structural grammar suggests others:

### 8.1 Piezoelectric Portal (No Cells)
Replace the biological sheath with a piezoelectric polymer (PVDF-TrFE) nanotube wrap. CSF pressure → voltage → embedded μLED array. No genetics, no cells, no immune considerations. The trade: no living tissue, no biological integration. Structural type shifts from 𐑧→𐑘 (slow→fast kinetics).

### 8.2 Optogenetic Portal (Neural Interface)
Instead of luciferase, express channelrhodopsin-2 (ChR2) in the fibroblasts. An external μLED fiber threaded alongside the shunt stimulates the sheath with 470 nm light → ChR2 depolarizes → Ca²⁺ influx → the sheath becomes a *light-receiving* organ. The shunt as optical antenna. This inverts the signal flow: light → pressure (optogenetic stimulation of surrounding tissue) rather than pressure → light.

### 8.3 Multi-Portal Entanglement (Ω: 𐑭→𐑟)
The structural gap identified in §5. Two shunt-bearers, each with an SPS. Their light pulses are entangled via quantum dot FRET pairs embedded in both sheaths. When one portal-bearer's CSF pressure changes, the other's light shifts — nonlocal correlation across bodies. This closes the final CLINK promotion to L8 (Whole Organism) and achieves the non-Abelian winding (𐑟) that the SPS currently lacks. The engineering is speculative but structurally specified.

### 8.4 Tattoo Portal (No Implant)
The simplest path: inject the SPS fibroblasts intradermally along the shunt path rather than wrapping the shunt. The cells form a linear tattoo of bioluminescent tissue *in* the skin directly overlying the shunt. No tunneling, no sheath fabrication, no implant — just a series of intradermal injections. The light traces the shunt from above. Structurally identical to the SPS but at L5 (Cell) rather than L7 (Tissue).
