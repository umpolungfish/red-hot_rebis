## 7. The Gut Microbiome: An Underexplored Fifth Mechanism

Dietary purines are not the only purine source — the gut microbiome metabolizes purines locally, and some bacterial species express uricase (which humans lost through evolution). The dietary pattern's $\text{{\igfont 𐑫}}$ chirality depends partly on microbiome adaptation:

- **Lactobacillus species** (from yogurt, kefir, fermented vegetables) can degrade purines in the gut lumen before absorption
- **Dietary fiber** (from vegetables, legumes, whole grains) promotes short-chain fatty acid production, which reduces systemic inflammation and improves renal function
- **Polyphenol metabolism** — gut bacteria convert chlorogenic acid to caffeic acid, anthocyanins to phenolic acids; individual variation in this conversion explains some heterogeneity in dietary response

**Recommendation:** Include fermented foods (yogurt, kefir, kimchi, sauerkraut) daily. The dietary pattern already provides substantial fiber — aim for 30+ g/day from diverse sources.
