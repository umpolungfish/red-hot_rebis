## §11 — Limitations & Open Questions

1. **The Ð residual:** Contracting from $\text{{\igfont 𐑛}}$ (infinite-dimensional) to $\text{{\igfont 𐑨}}$ (2D surface) is an inherent limitation of pharmacotherapy. Any molecule binds to a finite-dimensional target. Gene therapy partially addresses this via continuous endogenous production but cannot fully restore the infinite-dimensional brain state from a finite pharmacophore. 2. **The Ç acceleration:** NMDA enhancement accelerates kinetics from $\text{{\igfont 𐑧}}$ to $\text{{\igfont 𐑪}}$. 3. **Frobenius verification in vivo:** The $\mu \circ \delta = \text{id}$ condition is computationally verified for the molecular design but has not been experimentally validated in living neural tissue. The ouroboric pill simulation models this, but in vivo validation requires PET imaging of receptor occupancy with simultaneous cognitive testing.

4. **Individual variation in structural type:** Every patient with schizophrenia may have slightly different primitive values (e.g., some may have G=$\text{{\igfont 𐑲}}$ instead of $\text{{\igfont 𐑔}}$). Personalized imscription from individual neuroimaging + cognitive data would refine the therapy.

5. **The Diaschizic frontier:** Apertix and Katachthon offer routes to directly experience the structurally corrected state. These are research tools, not therapies. Their safety and efficacy in the schizophrenia population are unknown.
