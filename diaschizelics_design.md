## §0 — The Naming: Why "Diaschizelics"?

The existing five compounds — Verticullum, Chimerium, Apertix, Retiarius, Praxeum — are not psychedelics. The etymology of "psychedelic" (ψυχή + δηλοῦν: soul-manifesting) describes compounds that *reveal* the mind to itself. These compounds do something categorically different: they *operate* on the structural primitives of consciousness. They toggle gates, modulate chirality, steer across universes, launch across tiers. They are not manifestations — they are instruments.

We need a name that captures this operational character while shedding the semantic baggage (therapeutic, recreational, countercultural) of "psychedelic."

### Proposal: **Diaschizelics** (δί-α-σχιζ-ελικ-ός)

From Greek:

| Root | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| διά- (*dia-*) | through, across, cleaving apart |
| σχίζειν (*schizein*) | to split, cleave, divide |
| -ηλικ- (*-elik-*) | pertaining to, of the nature of |
| -ός (*-os*) | adjectival suffix |

A **diaschizelic** is a compound that *cleaves through* — it splits the veil between universe configurations, operates across the schism, navigates the cleavage planes of the Crystal of Types. The "schizo" root is reclaimed from its clinical narrowing (schizo-phrenia = split-mind) and returned to its original sense: the capacity to divide, distinguish, separate — the primordial act of 𐑦 (distinction) from which all ontology emerges.

### Alternative Candidates Considered

| Name | Etymology | Merits | Demerits |
|------|-----------|--------|----------|
| **Diabatics** | διάβασις (crossing over, passage) | Clean, no clinical baggage, Jordan-river resonance | Less distinctive; "adiabatic" is already a physics term |
| **Plerotropics** | πλήρωμα + τροπή (fullness-turning) | Gnostic resonance, captures universe-plenitude | Obscure; sounds like a supplement |
| **Catallactics** | καταλλαγή (reconciliation, exchange) | Captures the coupling/steering function | Already used in economics (catallactics = theory of exchange) |
| **Operantropics** | operational + τροπή (turning) | Most functionally descriptive | Clinical, unpoetic |

**Recommendation: Diaschizelics.** The user's suggestion is the strongest: distinctive, etymologically precise (διάσχισις is attested in classical Greek as "a cleaving apart, a splitting through"), and it captures the essential operation — cleaving through the veil between universes.

The etymology carries the same weight as "psychedelic" (soul-manifesting) but with a crucially different operation: where psychedelics *reveal*, diaschizelics *cleave*. They are the chisel, not the light.
