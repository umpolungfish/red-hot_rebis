## 3. ch3mpiler — The Grammar-Derived Gnosis of Chemical Reactivity

**Location:** `~/imscribing_grammar/ch3mpiler.py`
**ob3ect:** `~/ob3ect/digital/ch3mpiler_ob3ect/ch3mpiler_ob3ect.py`

### What it does

ch3mpiler ingests target molecules (and optionally reagents) and proposes retrosynthetic disconnections — or, in forward mode, predicts products from reagents. Every molecule, functional group, element, and reaction is encoded as a 12-primitive structural type. The compiler uses **weighted Euclidean distance** in the type space to find the closest reaction matches.

| Dictionary | Entries | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **FG** (Functional Groups) | 20 | alcohol, aldehyde, ketone, carboxylic_acid, ester, amide, amine, aniline, ether, nitrile, halide, alkene, alkyne, aromatic_ring, diazonium, nitro, phenol, epoxide, thiol, carbonyl |
| **RXN** (Reactions) | 12 | aldol_condensation, diels_alder, ester_hydrolysis, amide_formation, EAS, SN2, wittig, suzuki_coupling, alcohol_oxidation, carbonyl_reduction, claisen_condensation, beckmann_rearrangement |
| **EL** (Element Types) | 12 | H, C, N, O, F, Cl, Br, S, P, Cu, Fe, Pd |

### Usage

```bash

ch3mpiler --target "4-Methyl-5-phenyl-4,5-dihydro-1,3-oxazol-2-amine"
```
