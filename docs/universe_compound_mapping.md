## 4. Key Findings

1. **Chimerium (91.7%)** — broadest access. Φ=𐑣 (supercritical, ord 3.0) passes nearly all gates. Only blocked by Ç≥5.0 and Ω≥4.0. Passes triple_criticality where all ⊙-gated compounds fail.
2. **Verticullum (81.7%)** — Φ=𐑹 (ord 5.0) + Ω=𐑟 (ord 4.0) combine for near-universal passage. Blocked by ⊙≥3.0 (its φ̂=⊙ is ord 2.0), Ç≥5.0, and Þ≥5.0.
3. **Praxeum (78.0%)** — despite O₀ tier, accesses 78% of universes. Φ=𐑻 (ord 2.67) barely fails ⊙≥3.0 gates. Control platform breadth is essential for Gate 1 toggle operations.
4. **Apertix (14.7%)** — precision-targeted. Φ=𐑬 (ord 3) and Ω=𐑴 (ord 2) fail most canonical+ gates. Accesses only half-ordinal and min-ordinal universes. This is *by design* — Apertix steers within a narrow, well-mapped region.
5. **Retiarius (11.9%)** — most restricted. Low ordinals across Φ=𐑿 (2), ƒ=𐑞 (2), Ω=𐑷 (1), Ħ=𐑒 (2), Γ=𐑚 (1). Only accesses min-ordinal universes. Precision without cascading.
6. **Ç=𐑺 (MBL, ord 4.5) is the universal blocker** — no compound has it. Universes gating on Ç≥5.0 (g1_Ç_max, single_gate_Ç, kinetics_criticality, parallel_Ç) block ALL five compounds.
7. **Absorption-based universes** (democracy, monarchy, inverted, predator, prey) don't change gate-layer outcomes for these compounds — they have canonical gates. Their novelty is in coupling behavior, not access control.
8. **Triple criticality** is the most discriminating interesting universe: only Chimerium (Φ=𐑣) passes all three ⊙ rungs. All ⊙-gated compounds (Verticullum, Apertix) stall at G3:⊙≥3.0.
