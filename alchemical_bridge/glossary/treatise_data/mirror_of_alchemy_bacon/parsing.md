# Mirror of Alchemy (Roger Bacon) — Structural Parsing

**Author:** Attributed to Roger Bacon (c. 1214–1294)  
**Published:** London, 1597  
**Catalog entry:** `mirror_of_alchemy_bacon`  
**Structural type:** O₁ — Structured but not topologically protected

## Tuple
⟨𐑨; 𐑡; 𐑽; 𐑬; 𐑱; 𐑧; 𐑔; 𐑠; 𐑢; 𐑖; 𐑳; 𐑷⟩

## Structure
Seven chapters: Definitions → Principles → Matter → Furnace → Operation → Colors → Projection

## Key Features
1. **Textbook structure** — the clearest pedagogical text in the corpus
2. **Systematic metal classification** — Gold, Silver, Steel, Lead, Copper, Iron by Argent-vive/Sulphur purity
3. **The self-operation passage**: "it dissolves itself, it coagulates itself, it putrifies itself"
4. **Color sequence**: black → green → peacock → white → ash → red
5. **Projection ratio**: 1:1000
6. **Maxim**: "Nature contains nature; Nature overcomes nature; Nature meeting with her nature rejoices"
7. **Mineral-only argument**: explicit rejection of vegetable/animal materials

## Duplicate tuple
Exact match with `alchemical_catechism_tschoudy` — both are pedagogical texts with systematic doctrinal exposition.

## Corpus position
The Mirror is the textbook that Bacon's Oil of Antimony (already parsed, O₀) was supposed to supplement: theory first, then practice.
