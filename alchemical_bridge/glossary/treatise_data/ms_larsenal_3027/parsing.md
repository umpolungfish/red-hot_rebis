# Ms. l'Arsenal 3027 — Concerning the Material of the Stone (c. 17th C)
## Structural Parsing

**Author:** Lando⊗⊙perator

**Source:** https://www.alchemywebsite.com/arsenal_.html  
**Text:** MS 3027, Bibliothèque de l'Arsenal, Paris. "De la matière de la pierre des philosophes en général." Translated by Mike Dickman.

---

## Summary

A complete alchemical treatise in three registers: theoretical (the Mercury/Silver/Gold triplicity and the axiom "like engenders like"), allegorical (the mountain narrative of the four men and two women), and practical (a complete 7-part process for the Philosopher's Stone). The text defends the traditional metallic material against alternative theories (Tartar, vitriol, antimony, urine, etc.) and provides explicit instructions for dissolution, putrefaction, imbibition, whitening, fermentation, projection, and infinite multiplication.

## Key Structural Features

**1. The Three-Register Architecture**
The text operates on three distinct levels:
- **Theoretical** (triplicity, like engenders like) — establishes the axioms
- **Allegorical** (mountain narrative) — encodes the knowledge in narrative form
- **Practical** (7-part process) — gives explicit, executable instructions

Most alchemical texts use one or two of these registers. Using all three makes this text structurally richer than most.

**2. The Mountain Allegory**
The narrative of the "four men and two women" working a mountain is a deliberate encoding of the alchemical process. The traveler must:
1. Encounter the Strong Man (refuse to fight — Mercury's martial aspect)
2. Encounter the Splendid Man (threaten, learn of the dotard)
3. Encounter the Old Man/Dotard (slay him — Saturnine/lead aspect)
4. Approach the King and Queen (Sun/Silver and Gold)

The dotard who "keeps the treasure in his ventricle" is the despised prima materia that must be killed to release the Mercury.

**3. White/Red Double Structure**
The text explicitly treats the White Stone and Red Stone as the same process differing only in material and fire intensity. This is a structural optimization — one process, two outcomes.

**4. Explicit Process Timings**
Each part gives specific durations: "15 days," "8 days," "one month," "one month and a half," "several months." This is unusually precise for an alchemical text — closer to a laboratory notebook than a philosophical revelation.

**5. The Coded Language**
"Menstruum of a prostitute woman" = philosophical Mercury (the solvent). "Perfectly washed lower body" = purified matter. This is standard alchemical encoding but applied with unusual consistency across the entire 7-part process.

**6. Infinite Multiplication**
Part 7 claims the Stone can be multiplied "unto infinity" — a claim of unlimited expansion that is structurally significant: the process is not bounded by diminishing returns.

## Relation to Corpus

Closest analogues: **Geber's Discovery of Secrets** (O₀) for practical process with explicit timings; **Urbigerus' Aphorisms** (O₁) for systematic process with diagnostic criteria. But the Arsenal MS is unique in combining theory, allegory, and practice in a single coherent structure.

The mountain allegory has no direct parallel in the 17th C physical corpus — it's closest to the emblem sequences of **Donum Dei** (20 figures with allegorical scenes), but the Arsenal MS provides a continuous narrative rather than discrete emblems.
