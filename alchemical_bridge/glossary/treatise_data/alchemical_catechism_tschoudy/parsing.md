## Key Structural Features

### 1. Q&A Format — Catechetical Structure
The catechism is structurally unique in the corpus: systematic doctrinal exposition through question and answer. Each Q&A pair is a self-contained unit but builds cumulatively into a complete philosophical system.

### 2. The Water-Table Analogy
The most vivid passage: water poured from a central vase flows over colored substances and takes on their qualities — a model for how the universal seed is modified by the terrestrial channels it passes through. This is a **structural analogy for the coupling between universal principle and local instantiation**.

### 3. Centre and Ex-Centre
The seed is deposited at the "centre of the earth" (a void place) and expelled to the "ex-centre" (circumference). This center/periphery model maps onto the Ð-structure of the grammar — the tension between concentrated potential and distributed manifestation.

### 4. Relation to Turba
Distance 0.0 from Turba with single Ř conflict: adjoint (catechism) vs bidirectional (dialogue). These two texts represent opposite poles of the instructional spectrum — one is doctrinal transmission, the other is dialectical exchange.
