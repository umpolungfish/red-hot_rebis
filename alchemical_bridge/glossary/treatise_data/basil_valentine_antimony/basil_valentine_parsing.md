## 1. Text Overview

One of the most famous iatrochemical texts — a full-length book (~335,000 characters) devoted to a single substance: antimony. Unlike the process-oriented alchemical recipes, this is a **comprehensive medicinal-philosophical treatise** arguing for antimony as a universal medicine, attacking ignorant physicians, and embedding practical chemistry within a religious framework of prayer and contemplation.

The text has extensive Latin-translator commentary (in italics), creating a multi-layered reading experience where the original author and the editor speak simultaneously.

| Register | Content | Function |

| **Medical** | Internal vs. external diseases, humors, wounds | Gives practical medicinal framework |
| **Polemical** | Attack on "umbratical" doctors, defense of true knowledge | Establishes author's authority |
