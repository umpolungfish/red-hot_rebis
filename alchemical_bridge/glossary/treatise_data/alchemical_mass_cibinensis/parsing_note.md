## Key Structural Features
1. **Liturgical scaffolding**: Introitus → Kyrie → Graduale → Ave Maria (only four parts preserved in this extract)
2. **The Ethiopian**: The nigredo figure — "burned, calcined, discoloured, altogether dead and lifeless" — then "buried, sprinkled with his own moisture and slowly calcined till he shall arise"
3. **Bath of rebirth**: "Because of the bath of rebirth he takes a new name, which the philosophers call the natural sulphur"
4. **Three→One**: Moon, Mars, Mercury produce one child

## Historical Significance
Melchior Cibinensis (also known as Melchior of Hermannstadt, now Sibiu, Romania) was a 16th-century Hungarian/Saxon alchemist. The use of the Mass as a framework for alchemy represents a striking example of *theologia alchemica* — the complete synthesis of Christian liturgy and hermetic practice.
