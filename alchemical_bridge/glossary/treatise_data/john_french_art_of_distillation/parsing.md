---

## Summary

The most comprehensive practical manual in the 17th C physical corpus. Six books systematically covering distillation theory, compound waters, minerals, animals, spagyrical experiments, and the anatomy of gold and silver, with 42 woodcuts of furnaces and vessels. John French writes as a "Dr. of Physick" in the Paracelsian iatrochemical tradition, explicitly teaching the reader rather than concealing secrets. The lengthy preface is a vigorous defense of alchemy against skeptics and charlatans alike.

### Tier: O₁

**Justification:** John French's text is a systematic technical manual — more structured than a recipe collection (O₀) but without the self-referential or critical features of O₂. It is O₁ because:
1. **Systematic six-book organization** with thematic coherence
2. **Complete glossary** of operations defined in standardized language
3. **Pedagogical structure** — rules, definitions, illustrations
4. **Multi-kingdom scope** (mineral, vegetable, animal)
5. **Meta-textual awareness** — the preface is an apologia for the art itself, and French promises future volumes

French's text is structurally closest to a **modern chemistry textbook** — it teaches rather than initiates.

Plus: Epistle Dedicatory, Preface, 42 woodcuts of apparatus.

### The Preface as Apologia

The preface is its own structural genre — a **defense of alchemy** that covers:

1. **Attack on Galenist medicine** — "their strict observation of the quadruplicity of humours" is confuted by Paracelsus and Van Helmont
2. **Defense against skeptics** — answers the objection "why are not the possessors thereof infinitely rich?" with Artephius (1000 years), Flamel (14 hospitals), Bacon (miracles)
3. **Attack on charlatans** — denounces "sophisticating, cheating mountebanks" who sell fake oils and salts
4. **The five keys of Nollius** — a pedagogical framework for the student:
   1. Seek it from above with pious intent
   2. Learn theory before practice
   3. Read the true philosophers, not imposters
   4. Imitate nature in all things
   5. Acquaint yourself with a true philosopher as tutor
5. **Prophetic conclusion** — "the fourth monarchy which is northern is dawning... the Golden Age in which all tyranny, oppression, envy, and covetousness shall cease"
