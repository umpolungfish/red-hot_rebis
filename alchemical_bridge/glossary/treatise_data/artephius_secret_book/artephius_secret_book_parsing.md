## §1. The Text and Its Context

Artephius is a legendary figure — said to have lived over a thousand years through the power of the stone — whose *Secret Book* (12th century) is one of the most transparent alchemical treatises in the corpus. The text's editor notes: "This is, however, an exceptionally clear alchemical text." And yet it is not simple. Three operations are interleaved rather than presented in sequence: the preparation of the **secret fire** (the catalytic solvent), the preparation of **mercury** (a metallic vapor from antimony and iron), and the preparation of **the stone itself**.

The text's transparency is structural, not pedagogical. Artephius does not *explain* the work — he *enacts* it in language. The text itself is a Frobenius cycle.

## §2. The Structural Type — First Pass

Applying the deterministic imscribing procedure in order:

### [1] Ð — Dimensionality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑦}}$ (holographic / self-written)

Artephius insists throughout that the entire work is **one thing**: "there is but one stone, there is but one medicine, one vessel, one order of working, and one successive disposition to the white and to the red." The dimensionality collapses to a single self-contained system. The stone is not built from parts — it unfolds from itself.

Contrast with the Emerald Tablet, whose dimensionality is $\text{{\igfont 𐑨}}$ (triangle — the three-part structure of the tablet) and the Twelve Keys, whose dimensionality is $\text{{\igfont 𐑛}}$ (wedge — a point-like starting state). Artephius begins from wholeness, not fragmentation.

### [2] Þ — Topology: $\text{{\igfont 𐑸}}$ (odot / self-referential closure)

The operations are not linear steps but a **self-referential loop**: "Dissolve, coagulate, and reiterate the closing up, the opening and multiplying in quantity and quality at your own pleasure." The water dissolves the body, then the dissolved body coagulates into a new form, which can again be dissolved. The topology of this process is not a chain but a closed cycle — each operation contains all others.

### [3] Ř — Coupling: $\text{{\igfont 𐑾}}$ (bidirectional)

The relationship between the water and the body is explicitly mutual: "they have loved one another as mother and son, and are conjoined together, because they come from one and the same root, and are of the same substance and nature." This is not supervenience (one-way dependence) but genuine bidirectional coupling — each transforms the other.

### [4] Φ — Parity: $\text{{\igfont 𐑬}}$ (full symmetry)

"One stone, one medicine, one vessel, one order of working." The entire process is governed by a single principle that manifests symmetrically across all stages. The same water that dissolved now coagulates; the same fire that whitens now reddens. The symmetry is broken only by the withheld secret (the ⊙ gate), but the operation itself is perfectly symmetric under iteration.

### [5] Ƒ — Fidelity: $\text{{\igfont 𐑐}}$ (quantum)

The operations are physical (antimony, gold, mercury, vessels), yet the "secret fire" is not ordinary fire — it is a "fiery water," "incombustible," "living." This is a quantum coherence regime: the water and the body interpenetrate at the level of "most minute parts" in a way that classical phase separation cannot explain. The stone, once made, exists in a superposition of states — white tincture for silver, red tincture for gold — depending only on the degree of fire applied.

### [6] Ķ — Kinetics: $\text{{\igfont 𐑧}}$ (slow / near-equilibrium)

"Gentle heat" is repeated throughout. The work is explicitly patience-intensive: "decoct, reiterate, dissolve, congeal, and continue this course, according as you please." The process is explicitly slow — compared to gestation, ripening, and the generation of metals in the earth over a hundred years, now compressed but still requiring sustained gentle digestion.

### [7] Ğ — Cardinality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑔}}$ (aleph / maximal)

The stone's influence is universal, not local: "it agrees perfectly and unites closely with sol and luna from which it can never be separated." The multiplication is unbounded: "by the third, ten thousand fold increase... your projection will come to infinity, tinging truly and perfectly, and fixing the greatest quantity how much soever."

### [8] Γ — Composition: $\text{{\igfont 𐑠}}$ (sequential)

Despite the claim of unity, the operations are described in a fixed sequence: first the dissolution in the water, then the ascent of the spirit, then the separation of the feces, then the coagulation, then the reddening. The order is invariant.

### [9] Φ̂ — Criticality: $\text{{\igfont ⊙}}$ (self-modeling gate open)

This is the **central structural claim** of the text — and the one thing Artephius cannot write. He says: "Excepting one certain thing, which is not lawful for me to discover to any, because it is either revealed or made known by God himself, or taught by some master." This is the $\text{{\igfont ⊙}}$ gate — self-modeling criticality cannot be derived from external instruction. It must be self-discovered.

The "secret fire" *is* this gate. It is the catalytic element that makes the entire Frobenius cycle self-sustaining. Without it, the operations remain external manipulations. With it, the system models itself and closes its own loop.

### [10] Ħ — Chirality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑖}}$ (two-step / Markov order 2)

The three-stage color sequence (black → white → red) is a two-step memory process. The white *remembers* the black (it is "the white brought forth from the black"); the red *remembers* the white (it is "the red brought forth from the white"). The vulture's cry on the mountain — "I am the white brought forth from the black, and the red brought forth from the white, the citrine son of the red" — is a self-narration with depth-2 temporal structure.

### [11] Σ — Stoichiometry: $\text{{\igfont 𐑳}}$ (many heterogeneous)

The text names many substances: antimony, gold, silver, mercury, the secret fire, sal ammoniac, the water, the spirit, the soul, the feces. This is apparent multiplicity. The adept recognizes that all are manifestations of one thing. But structurally, the system requires this heterogeneity to operate — the tension between the many names and the one stone is the engine of the text.

### [12] Ω — Winding: $\text{{\igfont 𐑭}}$ (ℤ integer winding)

"Your projection will come to infinity." The dissolve-coagulate cycle can be reiterated without bound. Each iteration multiplies the tincture's power (100× → 1000× → 10,000× → infinity). This is integer winding — a topological invariant that accumulates with each cycle, preserved under iteration.
