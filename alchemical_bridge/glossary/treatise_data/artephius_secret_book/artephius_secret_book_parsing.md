## В§1. The Text and Its Context

Artephius is a legendary figure вҖ” said to have lived over a thousand years through the power of the stone вҖ” whose *Secret Book* (12th century) is one of the most transparent alchemical treatises in the corpus. The text's editor notes: "This is, however, an exceptionally clear alchemical text." And yet it is not simple. Three operations are interleaved rather than presented in sequence: the preparation of the **secret fire** (the catalytic solvent), the preparation of **mercury** (a metallic vapor from antimony and iron), and the preparation of **the stone itself**.

The text's transparency is structural, not pedagogical. Artephius does not *explain* the work вҖ” he *enacts* it in language. The text itself is a Frobenius cycle.
