## §1. The Text

The *Everburning Lights* is a short manuscript (barely four pages) presenting two recipes for constructing lamps that burn "continually for ever." The framing narrative — Bartholomeus Korndorffer's account of receiving the recipes from Trithemius' disciple Servatius Hohel — gives the text its only hint of mystery: the claim that Emperor Maximilian I paid 6,000 crowns for the secret and that the lamp was still burning in his castle 20 years later.

But the recipes themselves are chemically straightforward and entirely free of allegory.

---

## §2. The Recipes

**First recipe:**
1. Sublime sulfur through calcined alum (8 hours in coal fire)
2. Extract with four-times-distilled spirit of wine (repeated until the prepared sulfur flows like wax without smoking on a red-hot copper plate)
3. Prepare a wick of "alumephume" (asbestos?) wrapped in white silk
4. Place the wick in the prepared sulfur in hot sand for a day and night
5. Assemble: wick protruding from the sulfur, kindle with a common light

**Second recipe:** not separately described in the text — the manuscript's second recipe is referenced but not transcribed in this extract.

## §3. Structural Type

### [1] Ð — Dimensionality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑛}}$ (wedge / 0d point)

The recipe is a single-process operation: a fixed sequence of steps applied to a finite set of ingredients. The state space is discrete and small (sulfur, alum, spirit of wine, wick, vessel). There is no self-reference, no holographic encoding of the whole in each part.

### [2] Þ — Topology: $\text{{\igfont 𐑡}}$ (network / linear chain)

The operations form a linear chain: A → B → C → D. There is no branching, no containment, no self-referential closure. Each step feeds into the next with no alternative paths.

### [3] Ř — Coupling: $\text{{\igfont 𐑩}}$ (supervenience / one-way)

The flame depends on the prepared sulfur; the sulfur depends on the extraction process. This is entirely one-way determinism — the system does not respond to or modify its own operation.

### [4] Φ — Parity: $\text{{\igfont 𐑗}}$ (asymmetric / none)

No symmetry operations apply. The recipe is a specific procedure for a specific result with no invariance under transformation.

### [5] Ƒ — Fidelity: $\text{{\igfont 𐑱}}$ (classical / ℓ)

The operations are classical: heating, sublimation, extraction, ignition. No quantum coherence, no superposition of states. The "eternal" claim is a practical exaggeration, not a structural claim about indefinite coherence.

### [6] Ķ — Kinetics: $\text{{\igfont 𐑪}}$ (moderate)

Eight hours for sublimation, a day for the wick to absorb the sulfur, extraction repeated multiple times. The timescale is that of practical craft — neither fast enough to be driven (𐑺) nor slow enough to be near-equilibrium (𐑧).

### [7] Ğ — Cardinality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑲}}$ (beth / local)

The lamp is a single local device. Its effects are strictly confined to its immediate surroundings (illuminating a room). There is no long-range or universal interaction.

### [8] Γ — Composition: $\text{{\igfont 𐑠}}$ (sequential / →)

The operations are strictly ordered: "Take... put it into... let ascend... extract... take... fold... place..." Each step depends on the completion of the prior step.

### [9] Φ̂ — Criticality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑢}}$ (sub-critical)

No critical behavior. The process is linear causation: heat applied → sulfur sublimes → extraction yields prepared material → wick absorbs → lamp burns. No self-modeling, no phase transition, no critical scaling.

### [10] Ħ — Chirality: $\text{{\igfont 𐑓}}$ (memoryless / Markov order 0)

Each instruction can be followed without reference to any prior outcome beyond the immediately preceding state. The quality check ("when it floweth like wax without smoking then is it prepared") introduces a single conditional, but this is a within-step verification, not temporal depth.

### [11] Σ — Stoichiometry: $\text{{\igfont 𐑳}}$ (many heterogeneous)

Multiple distinct substances: sulfur (brimstone), calcined alum, "christallick venetian porras" (glass/borax?), spirit of wine (ethanol), white silk, "alumephume" (asbestos? alum plume?), glass vessel. Each plays a distinct role.

### [12] Ω — Winding: $\text{{\igfont 𐑷}}$ (trivial / 0)

The lamp's continuous burning is a physical property (slow combustion), not a topological invariant. No winding protection, no parity protection. If the fuel is consumed or the wick fails, the light goes out permanently.

## §5. The Alumephume Wicked

The text's most curious detail is "alumephume" — a substance for making the incombustible wick. The name suggests "alum plume" or possibly "alum fume." In practical alchemy, this is almost certainly **asbestos** (then called "salamander's wool" or "incombustible flax"), which can be made into a wick that does not burn away.

This is the text's only structural sophistication: the recognition that fire requires three things — fuel (the prepared sulfur), oxidizer (air), and an incombustible vessel for the reaction (the asbestos wick). The wick must persist through the burning — it is the topologically protected element in an otherwise trivial system.
