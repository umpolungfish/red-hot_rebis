## 1. Text Summary

A brief eyewitness account (~900 words) of a Polish physician who could cause phantom plants to grow from their own ashes inside sealed glasses. When heat was applied to the bottom of a glass containing plant ashes, "the manifest form of a Rose" would appear — stalk, leaves, flowers — growing "by little and little." When the heat was removed, the phantom collapsed back into ashes.

The author (Du Chesne) witnessed this and spent much time trying and failing to replicate it.

## 2. Structural Analysis

### The Phenomenon
A sealed glass containing plant ashes. Heat applied → phantom plant grows. Heat removed → phantom collapses. The plant is described as a "spirituall Idea, indued with a spiritual essence" — form without matter.

This is a **boundary phenomenon** between matter and information. The ashes encode the plant's morphogenetic structure; heat provides the energy to reconstruct it. The form is stored in the salt as pure structural memory.

### Key Features
- **Reversible**: plant appears and disappears with heat
- **Preserved form**: 30+ different plant species, each producing its correct shape
- **Witnessed but unreplicated**: the author saw it but could not do it
- **Threshold-gated**: heat on/heat off is the binary switch
