# Urbigerus — Circulatum Minus of Urbigerus (1690)
## Structural Parsing

**Author:** Lando⊗⊙perator  
**Date:** 2026-06-25  
**Source:** https://www.alchemywebsite.com/circulat.html  
**Text:** *Circulatum minus Urbigeranum, or the Philosophical Elixir of Vegetables, with The Three certain Ways of Preparing it, fully and clearly set forth on One and Thirty Aphorisms*. London, 1690.

---

## Summary

A companion text to the Aphorisms of Urbigerus, presenting 31 aphorisms on the **Vegetable Elixir** (Circulatum Minus) as distinct from the **Grand Elixir** (Circulatum Majus). The Vegetable Elixir is "specificated" to the Vegetable Kingdom only, can be prepared in one Philosophical Month, and is explicitly medicinal. Three ways are given, parallel to the three ways of the main Aphorisms but operating on different first matters.

### Tier: O₀

**Justification:** The Circulatum Minus is a practical recipe manual. Though it adopts the same aphoristic format as the main Aphorisms (O₁), it is domain-restricted (Vegetable only), shorter (31 vs 101 aphorisms), and thinner in theoretical content. Its three ways are more conventional and less structurally innovative. The text does not diagnose its own operation or contain meta-level reflection beyond the hierarchical Majus/Mininus distinction.

## Key Structural Features

**1. Hierarchical Elixir Typology**
The text explicitly defines its own position in a hierarchy: the Circulatum Minus is subordinate to the Circulatum Majus. This is the first text in the corpus to classify its own type within a multi-level system. The distinction is structural: Majus commands all three kingdoms, Minus is "restrained to one only Kingdom" (Aph. II).

**2. The Eternal Menstruum**
The Vegetable Menstruum "loses nothing of its Quantity or Quality" (Aph. II, XXV). This is the same property as the Universal Menstruum of the main Aphorisms but restricted to the Vegetable domain. The Menstruum is a **closed-loop system** — it can be recovered and reused indefinitely.

**3. The Bituminous Medium (Aph. VII)**
The third way requires a "sulphureous and bituminous Matter" known to "sea fishermen" — Copavian (from Copay, a fish), then Italian. This is the most **practical, specific ingredient** in the entire Urbigerus corpus. It grounds the text in a real substance from a real trade.

**4. The Instantaneous Extraction (Aph. I)**
The Circulatum Minus can "in a Moment extract" the true Essence of any Vegetable "without any Fire, or further Preparation." This is a claim of near-instantaneous operation — a dramatic speed contrast with the nine-month Grand Elixir.

**5. The Elixir Proprietatis (Aph. XXII)**
A specific preparation from Myrrh, Aloe, and Saffron — "the truest Elixir Proprietatis (as Paracelsus terms it)." This explicitly links Urbigerus to the Paracelsian iatrochemical tradition.

**6. Domain Restriction as Structural Principle**
The text's architecture is defined by what it excludes. The Circulatum Minus works only on Vegetables — it "will nevertheless in a moment draw the Tincture out of Metals and Minerals" but "will not separate all their Principles, not being the appropriate Menstruum" (Aph. XXIV). The limitation is structural, not practical.

## Relation to the Corpus

The Circulatum Minus is structurally closest to **Roger Bacon's Oil of Antimony** (O₀) — both are practical, single-preparation manuals. Its hierarchical relation to the main Aphorisms is unique in the corpus: no other text on this list explicitly defines itself as a subordinate companion.

The "Eternal Menstruum" concept anticipates the closed-loop alchemy of later texts, where the solvent is never consumed — a structural precursor to the conservation principles in 18th-century chemistry.
