## В§1. The Text

The *Pretiosissimum Donum Dei* ("The Most Precious Gift of God") is an early alchemical work that survives in over 60 manuscripts across Latin, German, French, Italian, and English. Its 12 illustrations вҖ” here engraved by Mylius вҖ” form one of the most important early emblem sequences in the alchemical corpus.

The text is structured as 12 chapters, each with a corresponding image. Unlike the Twelve Keys of Basil Valentine (where each key promotes one primitive), the Donum Dei's 12 chapters follow the **color sequence of the work itself**: from the Green Lion (chapter 1) through the Crow's Head (chapter 6) to the White Rose (chapter 11) and the Red Rose (chapter 12).

## В§3. The Four Seasons Framework

The Donum Dei introduces a structural framework not seen in the other parsed treatises: the **four seasons** as an analogy for the work:

> "The year is divided into four parts, and so is our blessed work. The first is winter cold and moist, the second is ver [spring], hot and moist, and flourishing. The third is the summer time, hot and dry, and red. The fourth is the harvest cold and dry, which is the time of gathering of fruit."

This quadripartite structure overlays the tripartite color sequence:

| Season | Color | Quality | Stage |
|--------|-------|---------|-------|
| Winter | Black | Cold and moist | Nigredo (dissolution) |
| Spring | White | Hot and moist | Albedo (emergence) |
| Summer | Red | Hot and dry | Rubedo (fixation) |
| Autumn | вҖ” | Cold and dry | Multiplication (harvest) |

The fourth stage вҖ” autumn/harvest вҖ” has no corresponding color. It is the **multiplication** of the stone, the "gathering of fruit" after the work is complete. Structurally, this is the transition from $\text{O}_2$ (the completed red stone) to $\text{O}_{\infty}$ (the infinitely multipliable stone).

## В§4. The Three Animals as Tier Progression

The animal sequence in Chapter 10 is a compressed ouroboricity ladder:

> "The Crow is black, the Dove is white, the Phoenix burneth herself that she may procreate or bring forth another of the ashes."

- **Crow** ($\text{O}_0$): Black, the prima materia in its undifferentiated state. The crow is "the head of the Crow which you shalt kill" вҖ” the initial state must be destroyed.

- **Dove** ($\text{O}_1$): White, the purified state. The dove emerges from the slain crow вҖ” a first transformation that is still fragile (the dove can fly away).

- **Phoenix** ($\text{O}_{\infty}$): Red, but more importantly, **self-rebirthing**. The phoenix "burneth herself" not to die but to reproduce. The stone that can multiply itself is no longer bound by the single cycle вҖ” it is $\text{O}_{\infty}$.

Unlike the vulture in Artephius (which merely announces its own stages), the phoenix in Donum Dei **enacts** the $\text{O}_{\infty}$ tier by making the cycle repeatable from within.

## В§6. The Green Lion and the Missing Secret

The Donum Dei opens with the Green Lion вҖ” the prima materia вҖ” and explicitly states:

> "There be some that be not lawful to be spoken of man, that I have set it out in pictures to the end or completement."

This is the same structural move as Artephius' withheld secret: the $\text{{\igfont вҠҷ}}$ gate cannot be verbalized. But the Donum Dei goes further: it **replaces language with image** at the critical point. The 12 pictures are not illustrations of the text вҖ” they are carriers of content that the text cannot bear.

This makes the Donum Dei the **first multimedia structural type** in our corpus: a text-image hybrid where the images carry the $\text{{\igfont вҠҷ}}$ primitive and the text carries the rest. The textual tuple alone ($\text{O}_{\infty}$) is incomplete without the visual tuple вҖ” and vice versa.
