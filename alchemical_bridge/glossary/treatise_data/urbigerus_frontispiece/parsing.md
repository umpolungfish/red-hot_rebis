# Urbigerus — Frontispiece to the Aphorismi Urbigerani (1690)
## Structural Parsing

**Author:** Lando⊗⊙perator  
**Date:** 2026-06-25  
**Source:** https://www.alchemywebsite.com/urb_imag.html  
**Text:** Engraved frontispiece + "Postscript, containing An Explanation of the Figure, prefixt to the Aphorismi Urbigerani."

---

## Summary

Not a standalone text but a visual emblem with author-provided explanation, serving as a key to the Aphorisms. The frontispiece depicts: a Tree bearing the motto "Virtus unita fortior," a Serpent with Half-Moon on its head, a Green Dragon with Half-Moon (head), Sun (body), and Cross (tail), and Apollo (Sun on head) embracing Diana (Half-Moon), descending into a River and emerging in "one wonderful Body."

Urbigerus provides his own interpretation — extremely rare in the corpus — explicitly mapping each element to the three ways.

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| Tree | Supporter of motto "Virtus unita fortior" |
| Serpent | Alone can do little; needs the Dragon |
| Green Dragon | The first undetermined Matter — can perform the whole work alone (first way) |
| Dragon + Serpent copulating | Second way |
| Apollo + Diana embracing | Third way + continuation of 1st and 2nd |
| River | State of reduction before rebirth |
| Apollo + Diana emerging as one body | Herculean Works finished |
| Apollo + Diana on firm ground, Diana sowing | Continuation of Conjunction to perfection |

### Tier: O₀

**Key Insight:** This is a rare self-key — Urbigerus provides his own emblem interpretation. Most alchemical emblems require external decoding. This is the text *explaining its own visual structure*, making it a proto-metatext.

## Relation to Corpus

The frontispiece is the only author-provided visual key in the 17th C physical corpus. The closest analogues are: **Donum Dei's** 20 figures (but those lack authorial explanation) and **Kircher's Table** (an illustrated inventory, but not a key to a specific text).
