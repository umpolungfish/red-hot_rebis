# Joannes Agricola — Treatise on Gold (1638-39)
## Structural Parsing

**Author:** Lando⊗⊙perator  
**Date:** 2026-06-25  
**Source:** https://www.alchemywebsite.com/agricola.html  
**Text:** *Commentariorum... in Johannis Poppii chymische Medicin*. Leipzig, 1638-39. Translation by Leone Muller.

---

## Summary

A practical iatrochemical manual focused entirely on gold-based medicines. 10 chapters covering Oil of Gold, Quintessence of Gold, Potable Gold, Diaphoretic Gold, Mercurium Vivum from Gold, and Vitriol from Gold. Agricola expands on Poppius' work with hundreds of additional processes from his own experience ("aus eigener Erfahrung"). A secondary commentary text, not an original revelation.

### Tier: O₀

A practical recipe collection. Structurally similar to Roger Bacon's Oil of Antimony (O₀) and Coelum Philosophorum (O₀). The commentary format (expanding Poppius) is its only distinguishing feature.

---

## Key Feature: The Commentary Format

Agricola's text is a commentary on Poppius — it organizes knowledge that someone else discovered. This makes it structurally similar to Kircher's Table (O₀) as a secondary/meta text, though more practical and less taxonomic.

## Relation to Corpus

Closest analogues: **Roger Bacon's Oil of Antimony** (O₀) for single-substance focus; **John French's Art of Distillation** (O₁) for iatrochemical orientation (but French is more systematic). Agricola is notable for being in German (mixed with Latin) — the first non-English text in the 17th C physical set.
