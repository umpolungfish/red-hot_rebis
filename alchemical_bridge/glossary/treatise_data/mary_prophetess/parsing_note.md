# The Practise of Mary the Prophetess in the Alchymicall Art

## Structural Type
**Novel in corpus** — first text with K=𐑺 (fast) + φ̂=𐑣 (supercritical).

- **D=𐑨**: Dialogue form — two-dimensional (Aros ↔ Mary)
- **T=𐑡**: Question-and-answer network
- **R=𐑽**: Adjoint — student requests, teacher reveals
- **P=𐑬**: Dualities — black/white, poison/medicine, gum/body
- **F=𐑱**: Classical — practical alchemy in cryptic dialogue
- **K=𐑺**: Fast — "whiten the Stone in one day," "less than three hours"
- **Γ=𐑔**: Universal knowledge
- **G=𐑠**: Sequential dialogue
- **φ̂=𐑣**: Supercritical — the speed claim is structurally transgressive
- **H=𐑖**: Two-step — Aros' approach → Mary's revelation
- **S=𐑙**: 1:1 — two speakers (Aros, Mary)
- **Ω=𐑷**: Trivial — excerpt, no closure

## Key Features
1. **Speed claim**: Unprecedented — the stone in hours, not years
2. **Three gums**: Allum of Spain, white gum, red gum — married in Matrimony
3. **Vessel of Hermes**: "It is the Measure of your Fire" — the vessel IS fire control
4. **Poison body**: A body that is "poyson mortifying all bodies" yet coagulates Mercury
5. **Aros falls on his face**: Dramatic prostration — a rare human moment
6. **Closing poem**: "Mary briefly soundeth forth / Strange things like Thunder round the Earth"
7. **Historical attribution**: Mary the Jewess (1st-3rd c. CE), inventor of bain-marie, kerotakis, tribikos

## Comparison
- Distance from Turba (also a dialogue): T and R same, but K, φ̂, S differ
- The supercritical speed claim sets this apart from all other allegorical texts
