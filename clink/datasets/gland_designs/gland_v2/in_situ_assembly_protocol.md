# SYNTHETIC DETOX GLAND — In-Situ Assembly Protocol v2.0
## Zero-Incision Percutaneous Injection | Ultrasound-Guided
**Author:** Lando ⊗ ⊙perator

## Overview

Instead of surgical implantation of a pre-formed organoid, the gland is **grown in place** within the omental fat pad. A 2.5 mL cell-hydrogel suspension (the "gland seed") is injected through a **22G spinal needle** under **ultrasound guidance**. The suspension gels at body temperature within seconds, and the gland self-assembles over 21 days via in-situ angiogenesis.

**Key advantages over v1.0 (surgical implantation):**
- ✅ Zero incisions — single needle puncture only
- ✅ No general anesthesia — local lidocaine
- ✅ No vascular anastomosis — vessels grow themselves
- ✅ Outpatient procedure — 15 minutes, go home same day
- ✅ No systemic immunosuppression — microencapsulation + local Treg induction
- ✅ No surgical scars
- ✅ Repeatable — can inject additional gland seeds if needed

## Prerequisites (Days -21 to -1)

### Patient Pre-Screening
- Complete blood count + metabolic panel
- Liver function (ALT, AST, GGT, bilirubin)
- Coagulation profile (PT, PTT, INR)
- Abdominal ultrasound (confirm omental fat pad thickness ≥ 2 cm)
- Allergic sensitivity screen to alginate, Pluronic F127, and antidote components
- Immunotyping (baseline Treg count, anti-HLA antibodies)

### Cell Product Preparation (Days -7 to -1)

The injectable gland seed is prepared from **cryopreserved master cell banks** and must be formulated fresh within 2 hours of injection.

**Day -7:** Thaw and expand cell lines:
- SENS-01 (Sensor cells): 5 × 10⁷ target
- PROD-01 (Producer cells): 8.5 × 10⁸ target
- SUP-01 (Vascular support): 5 × 10⁷ target
- EPC-01 (Endothelial progenitors): 5 × 10⁷ target

**Day -3:** Begin microencapsulation process (high-throughput microfluidics)

**Day -2:** Quality control: encapsulation efficiency >95%, viability >90%, functional sensor testing (add 10 μM paraoxon → confirm NF-κB activation by luciferase reporter in parallel QC aliquot)

**Day -1:** Prepare hydrogel precursors and growth factor payload. Store all components at 4°C.

**Day 0 (Procedure Day):** Load syringe as described in [Injectable Formulation](injectable_formulation.md). Transport on ice to procedure room.

## Injection Procedure (Day 0 — 15 minutes)

### Equipment Setup

| Item | Specification | Purpose |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Ultrasound machine | Linear array, 7.5-12 MHz | Visualize omental fat pad |
| Sterile probe cover | Long, with gel | Aseptic ultrasound guidance |
| Spinal needle | 22G × 3.5 inch, with stylet | Reach omentum through abdominal wall |
| Syringe | 5 mL Luer-Lok | Contains gland seed formulation |
| Extension tubing | 30 cm, low dead volume | Connect syringe to needle |
| Local anesthetic | Lidocaine 1%, 5 mL | Skin + subcutaneous tract |
| Marking pen | Surgical skin marker | Mark entry point |

### Step-by-Step Procedure

**Step 1: Positioning**
- Patient supine, arms above head
- Ultrasound probe positioned in left upper quadrant
- Identify: liver edge (superior), splenic flexure (left), transverse colon (inferior)
- Target: omental fat pad between stomach and transverse colon
- Depth from skin: typically 3-5 cm (varies with BMI)
- Confirm: omental fat pad thickness ≥ 1.5 cm at injection site

**Step 2: Skin Preparation**
- Mark entry point on skin with ultrasound guidance
- Sterile prep: chlorhexidine-alcohol scrub (30 second contact time)
- Sterile drape with fenestration at entry point

**Step 3: Local Anesthesia**
- Lidocaine 1%, 5 mL total
- 2 mL intradermal wheal at entry point
- 3 mL injected along anticipated needle tract (subcutaneous → muscle → peritoneum)
- Aspirate before each injection to confirm no intravascular placement

**Step 4: Needle Insertion**
- Remove stylet from spinal needle
- Attach extension tubing + syringe (pre-loaded gland seed on ice)
- Insert needle through skin at 45° angle, bevel up
- Advance under continuous ultrasound visualization:
  - **Skin → SubQ:** Visible as hyperechoic line through subcutaneous fat
  - **Muscle (rectus abdominis/external oblique):** Resistive pop, patient may feel pressure
  - **Peritoneum:** Second pop, visible as tenting of peritoneal line
  - **Omental fat:** Needle tip appears as bright dot within echogenic omental fat
- **Confirm position:** Needle tip should be 1.5-2 cm deep within omental fat pad, away from visible vessels (color Doppler to confirm)

**Step 5: Injection**
- Prime extension tubing (waste ~0.1 mL)
- Begin slow, steady injection at 0.5 mL/min
- **Total volume:** 2.5 mL
- The injected material appears as a **hypoechoic blob** expanding within the hyperechoic omental fat
- Observe: the blob should not leak, not track along tissue planes, and remain localized
- If resistance increases suddenly: stop, retract 2 mm, attempt again (may be in vessel or fascial plane)
- Target final blob dimensions: ~20 × 15 × 12 mm (matches target gland volume)

**Step 6: Needle Removal**
- Wait 30 seconds after injection completes (allows fibrin gel to set)
- Remove needle slowly, with negative pressure on syringe to prevent tract leakage
- Apply gentle pressure to puncture site for 30 seconds
- Apply sterile bandage

### Post-Procedure (Day 0)

| Time | Action | Observation |
|------|--------|-------------|
| 0-30 min | Observation in clinic | Puncture site dry; patient tolerating |
| 30 min | Ultrasound check | Confirm gel blob in place, no hematoma, no free fluid |
| 1 hour | Discharge home | Written instructions; emergency contact |
| 24h | Phone follow-up | Pain score, fever, nausea, swelling |

### Expected Side Effects (self-limiting, days 1-3)
- Mild abdominal discomfort at injection site (Tylenol as needed)
- Low-grade inflammatory response (mild CRP elevation — expected from hydrogel foreign body reaction)
- No limitations on activity; may return to work next day

## Functional Verification (Day 21)

### Toxin Challenge Protocol (Outpatient)

1. Baseline blood draw: antidote level, complete toxin panel, inflammatory markers
2. Administer **oral paraoxon** (0.1 mg/kg — simulates organophosphate exposure) — this is 0.1× LD50, safe in monitored setting
3. Serial blood draws at 0, 15min, 30min, 1h, 2h, 4h, 8h, 24h
4. Measure: antidote levels (ELISA), paraoxon levels (LC-MS/MS)
5. **Criteria for success:**
   - Antidote rises from baseline to >50 ng/mL within 4 hours
   - Paraoxon clearance half-life ≤ 2 hours (vs. 8-12 hours without gland)
   - No significant inflammatory response (CRP < 50 mg/L, procalcitonin < 0.5)

## Failure Modes and Contingencies

| Failure Mode | Detection | Intervention |
|-------------|-----------|-------------|
| Gel blob dissociates (injection into wrong plane) | Immediate ultrasound: material tracking | Aspirate if possible; repeat injection at correct site in 1 week |
| Insufficient vascularization | Day 14 Doppler: <30% perfusion | Subcutaneous G-CSF (mobilizes EPCs) + local VEGF injection |
| Capsule fibrosis (foreign body reaction) | Day 21: gland smaller than expected, hyperechoic | Short course oral dexamethasone (3 days); repeat in 2 weeks |
| Immune rejection (encapsulation failure) | Rising anti-HLA antibodies, declining antidote | Oral tacrolimus rescue; consider explanation via laparoscopic retrieval |
| Antidote underproduction | Day 21 toxin challenge: <50 ng/mL peak | Re-inject booster gland seed (5×10⁷ producer cells only) |
