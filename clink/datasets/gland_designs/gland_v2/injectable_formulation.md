# SYNTHETIC DETOX GLAND — Injectable Formulation v2.0
## Zero-Incision Gland Seed | In-Situ Self-Assembly
**Author:** Lando ⊗ ⊙perator

## Overview

The gland seed is a **2.5 mL liquid suspension** that is injected through a **22G needle** into the greater omentum under **ultrasound guidance**. At body temperature, it forms a stable hydrogel scaffold within 30 seconds. The cells — pre-loaded with all genetic circuits for toxin sensing and antidote production — self-assemble into the functional gland over 14-21 days as host blood vessels infiltrate the scaffold.

**No incisions. No laparoscopic ports. No general anesthesia. No sutures.**

### Phase 2: Microencapsulation (Cell Protection Layer)

Each cell is individually encapsulated in **alginate-PEG** before incorporation into the injectate. This protects the xenograft/human cells from host immune rejection without systemic immunosuppression.

**Microcapsule specifications:**
- **Diameter:** 100 ± 15 μm (uniform by microfluidic jetting)
- **Shell:** Ultrapure alginate (LVG, <100 endotoxin U/g) + 4-arm PEG-NHS (10 kDa)
- **Core:** Cell + 50% v/v Matrigel for viability
- **Pore cutoff:** ~100 kDa (passes nutrients, O₂, antidote protein; blocks IgG, immune cells)
- **Coating:** PLL (poly-L-lysine) outer layer for mechanical stability
- **Yield:** 95% encapsulation efficiency; 92% viability post-encapsulation

### Phase 3: Hydrogel Precursors (Scaffold System)

**Component A — Thermosensitive base gel (1.0 mL):**
- Pluronic F127 (Poloxamer 407): 20% w/v in cold PBS
- Hyaluronic acid-tyramine conjugate: 5 mg/mL (high MW, 1.5 MDa)
- RGD peptide (GRGDSP): 1 mM for cell adhesion
- **Behavior:** Liquid at 4°C → gel at ≥28°C (instantaneous at 37°C)
- **Mechanism:** Micellar packing of PPO-PEO-PPO triblock copolymer

**Component B — Enzymatic secondary crosslinker (0.2 mL):**
- Horseradish peroxidase (HRP): 10 U/mL
- H₂O₂: 0.01% v/v (catalytic amount)
- **Function:** Crosslinks HA-tyramine via HRP/H₂O₂ within 5 min at 37°C
- **Result:** Secondary covalent network reinforces the thermoreversible gel, preventing dissolution

**Component C — Fibrin rapid stabilizer (0.3 mL):**
- Fibrinogen (human plasma derived): 10 mg/mL
- Thrombin: 50 U/mL (activated just before injection)
- **Function:** Forms fibrin mesh within 15 seconds of injection — prevents cell settling and provides immediate mechanical integrity

## Syringe Preparation Protocol

### Materials

- 5 mL Luer-Lok syringe (sterile)
- 22G × 3.5 inch spinal needle (with stylet) — reaches omental fat pad
- Extension tubing (sterile, 30 cm, low dead volume)
- Three-way stopcock
- Ice bath for syringe (maintain at 4°C until injection)

### Loading Procedure (at bedside, <5 min)

1. **Pre-cool** syringe and extension tubing to 4°C
2. **Load Components B+C** (enzymatic crosslinkers + fibrin) into 0.5 mL dead space of extension tubing
3. **Load Components A+D+E** (gel + cells + growth factors + immune modulators) into main syringe barrel — total 2.0 mL
4. **Attach** extension tubing to syringe via stopcock
5. **Keep on ice** until moment of injection (max 2 hours)
6. **Prime** the line just before injection — the mixed components co-flow into the needle

### Injection Dynamics

When the mixed solution exits the needle into the omental fat pad (37°C):

1. **0-15 seconds:** Fibrin polymerizes (thrombin + fibrinogen) → immediate mechanical stabilization
2. **15-30 seconds:** Pluronic F127 reaches gelation temperature → micellar gel forms
3. **1-5 minutes:** HRP/H₂O₂ crosslinks HA-tyramine → secondary covalent reinforcement
4. **10-60 minutes:** Growth factors begin diffusing into surrounding tissue
5. **24-72 hours:** VEGF gradient established; host capillary sprouts visible at gel periphery
