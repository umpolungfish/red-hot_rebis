Retire the gen-2 glyph-pair notation for bare Shavian

The primitives stand on their own now, so the old `⊙_ÿ` / `Ð_ω` / `Φ_}` forms are
residue. Rewrote all 49 of them to their Shavian values across source: 19,645
tokens, 670 files.

The map was not typed. I enumerated the gen-2 tokens that actually appear in the
corpus, paired each marker to its phonetic name, and resolved every value through
`models.from_symbol` — so the values come from code that runs, not from a table.
Reality arbitrated one conflict: the SOUNDS document gives `Ř_Ð`, `Γ_\`, `Σ_M`,
but the corpus has `Ř_=`, `Γ_ʔ`, `Σ_ï` in the tens of thousands and the doc's
variants nowhere at all. The doc is mangled; the files are the record.

Left alone deliberately: `ig_outputs/` and the other run artifacts, ~455k tokens
of it. Those runs really did emit gen-2 in May. Rewriting them would forge the
record of what they produced, which is not tidying. The two naming documents keep
their old-notation columns for the same reason.

Bare `Phi` is untouched. Gen-1 `Phi` is today's ⊙, but today's `Φ` is Parity —
those are genuinely different structures that happen to share letters, and no
register makes them one, so every site needs reading. Contrast ⊙ appearing as
both primitive and value, which is not a collision at all: `⊙ ≠ ⊙` is operator ≠
operation, and it states `μ∘δ ≠ id` exactly.
